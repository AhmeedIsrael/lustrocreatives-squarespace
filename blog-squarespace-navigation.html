Creating a Sleek Navigation with Dropdowns in Squarespace

Squarespace TipsJanuary 20265 min read

Navigation is the backbone of your website's user experience. A well-structured navigation menu helps visitors find what they're looking for quickly and keeps them engaged. In Squarespace, creating a sleek dropdown navigation is easier than you might think — but there are important design principles to get right.

Why Navigation Matters More Than You Think

Studies consistently show that 94% of first impressions are design-related, and your navigation is one of the first elements visitors interact with. A cluttered or confusing menu can drive potential customers away before they even see your content.

Good navigation should be:

  • Intuitive — visitors should find what they need in under 3 clicks
  • Clean — avoid overwhelming users with too many options
  • Consistent — maintain the same structure across all pages
  • Accessible — work perfectly on mobile, tablet, and desktop

Setting Up Dropdown Menus in Squarespace

Squarespace makes it straightforward to create dropdown navigation. Here's the step-by-step process:

  1. Open your Pages panel in the Squarespace editor
  2. Create a folder by clicking the "+" icon and selecting "Folder"
  3. Name your folder — this becomes the parent menu item
  4. Drag pages into the folder — these become your dropdown items
  5. Reorder as needed — drag to arrange the dropdown order
💡 Pro Tip

Keep your top-level navigation to 5-7 items maximum. Anything more causes decision paralysis. Use dropdowns to organise sub-pages under logical parent categories.

Custom Styling Your Dropdown

While Squarespace provides default dropdown styling, custom CSS can elevate your navigation from good to exceptional. Here are some popular enhancements:

  • Smooth animations — add slide-down or fade-in transitions
  • Background blur — glassmorphism effects for a modern look
  • Active state indicators — highlight the current page in the menu
  • Mega menus — multi-column dropdowns for content-heavy sites

Mobile Navigation Best Practices

Over 60% of web traffic comes from mobile devices one of the reasons why your mobile navigation needs to be flawless. On Squarespace, the mobile menu automatically converts to a hamburger menu — but there are details worth fine-tuning:

  • Ensure tap targets are at least 44px × 44px for accessibility
  • Use clear, descriptive labels (avoid icon-only navigation)
  • Test dropdown depth — deeply nested menus are frustrating on mobile
  • Add a prominent CTA button in the mobile menu

The best navigation is the one users don't notice. It should feel natural, guiding visitors exactly where they want to go without them thinking about it.

Common Navigation Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Too many top-level items — keep it to 5-7 max
  2. Vague labels — "Services" is better than "What We Do"
  3. Missing CTA — always include a clear action button
  4. Ignoring mobile — test on real devices, not just browser resize
  5. No visual hierarchy — use weight and colour to guide the eye
